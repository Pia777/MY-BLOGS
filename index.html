An 8% overall conversion rate from website visit to order is strong for this category — the industry average for considered D2C home goods sits at 3–6%. Reaching it requires deliberate optimisation at every stage. Below is how Mitti & Co. approaches each one.

02
Top of Funnel — Building Awareness for a New Category

The hardest part of selling sustainable crockery is that most buyers don't yet know they have a problem. The goal of Mitti & Co.'s top-of-funnel is not just brand awareness — it is problem awareness. The brand must first make someone care about BPA before it can sell them a solution.

📱
Paid Social
Instagram & Meta Ads
Short-form video content that leads with the problem — "Did you know most food containers release plasticisers when heated?" — before introducing the product. Hook-first creative.
Target: ₹18 CPM · 3.2% CTR
🎥
Organic Content
Reels & YouTube Shorts
The brand creates a content series: "What's in your kitchen?" — testing common containers for toxins, comparing materials, showing the difference. Shared and saved heavily by the target audience.
Target: 500K monthly reach by month 9
🤝
Influencer
Micro-Influencers (10K–80K)
Partnerships with food bloggers, nutritionists, and clean-living creators who already have an audience primed to care. Not celebrity endorsements — credible, niche voices with high trust.
Target: 40 partners · ₹6,000 avg fee
🔍
SEO & Content
Search Intent Articles
60 long-form articles targeting queries like "BPA-free food containers India", "is plastic tiffin safe for kids", "best glass storage containers". Captures high-intent, research-mode buyers.
Target: 22,000 organic visits/month by month 12

03
Mid-Funnel — Converting Curiosity into Intent

Someone lands on the Mitti & Co. website having seen a reel about plastic toxicity in lunchboxes. They're curious but not convinced. The mid-funnel has one job: turn that curiosity into enough confidence to add to cart.

🧪
Trust & Proof Layer
Drop risk: HIGH
▼

A sustainable product brand with no physical retail presence must earn trust digitally. Every page element is a trust signal.

🏅Lab certifications front and centre — BPA-free, food-grade, FSSAI-compliant certificates on every product page, not buried in an FAQ.
🎬Product story video — A 90-second video on the homepage showing how the product is made, what it's made from, and why it matters. Reduces bounce rate by establishing brand credibility.
⭐Review architecture — At least 200 reviews per hero product before scaling ads. Verified buyer reviews with photos. Negative reviews visible and responded to.
📰Press coverage — Features in Healthshots, The Better India, Economic Times on sustainability brands. Logos displayed as social proof.
📧
Email & WhatsApp Nurture
Drop risk: MEDIUM
▼

68% of first-time visitors don't buy on the first session. Capture intent with a lead magnet and nurture over 5–7 days.

📥Lead magnet: "The Plastic-Free Kitchen Starter Guide" — a downloadable PDF swapping common plastic items for safer alternatives. Captures email at 0% purchase commitment.
✉️5-email welcome series: Day 0 (the guide), Day 2 (the science of BPA), Day 4 (how Mitti products solve it), Day 6 (customer transformation story), Day 8 (first-order discount).
💬WhatsApp opt-in: Customers who add to cart but don't purchase get a WhatsApp nudge 6 hours later. Conversion rate from this touchpoint: 11–14% in comparable D2C brands.
🛒
Cart & Checkout Optimisation
Drop risk: VERY HIGH
▼

Checkout abandonment is the single biggest revenue leak in D2C. For a ₹2,800 AOV product, the barrier is psychology as much as price.

🎁Bundle anchoring: Default product page recommends a starter kit (₹3,499) over single items. Feels like more value, increases AOV, reduces per-unit shipping hesitation.
🚚Free shipping threshold: Set at ₹1,499 — just below the lowest single product. Incentivises adding one more item to cross the threshold.
💳No-cost EMI: Partner with Simpl and ZestMoney. A ₹2,800 order at ₹233/month removes the psychological barrier of the full price tag.
🔒Trust signals at checkout: SSL lock, "30-day no-questions return", COD option visible before payment step. Reduces late-stage anxiety.
04
Bottom of Funnel — Post-Purchase Is Where Real D2C Revenue Lives

In a sustainable FMCG brand, the first order is rarely the most profitable. A customer who buys a starter set and has a good experience will replace every piece of plastic in their kitchen over 12 months. Repeat purchase rate is the defining metric.

Without a Retention System
One-time purchase, no follow-up
Customer receives product and goes silent
No cross-sell to rest of product range
No referral ask — lost word-of-mouth potential
LTV: ~₹2,800 (one order)
With a Retention System
Day 3 post-delivery: "How are you liking it?" WhatsApp check-in
Day 14: Email featuring complementary products to what they bought
Day 30: Referral ask — "Gift a friend ₹300 off, you get ₹300"
Day 60: Replenishment nudge + seasonal bundle offer
LTV: ~₹9,200 over 12 months (3.3× first order)

The math is simple: acquiring a new customer for a sustainable D2C FMCG brand costs ₹600–₹900 in paid media. Retaining that customer costs ₹40 in email and WhatsApp automation. Every repeat purchase is essentially a 95% margin activity.

06
What This Funnel Teaches Any D2C Sustainable FMCG Brand
01 Sell the problem before the product. In a new category, your first marketing job is to make people care about BPA, microplastics, or food safety — not to sell your brand. Problem-aware buyers convert at 4× the rate of brand-aware buyers.
02 Education is your most scalable asset. A 90-second explainer video, a 1,500-word SEO article, or a 5-part email series keeps working for years. A paid ad stops the moment you stop paying. Build content infrastructure before scaling ad spend.
03 Mid-funnel is where most D2C brands leak. Awareness and checkout get all the attention. The real opportunity is the space between — the customer who visited twice, added to cart, and then disappeared. Email and WhatsApp nurture is the cheapest funnel fix available.
04 AOV and LTV matter more than conversion rate. A 2% CVR with ₹9,200 LTV is more valuable than a 6% CVR with ₹2,800 LTV. Design your funnel around the long-term customer, not the first transaction.
05 For a sustainability brand, community is a channel. Customers who believe in the mission recruit for you. Build a referral loop, celebrate customer stories, create a sense of shared identity. Your values are your most defensible competitive advantage.
06 Post-purchase is the beginning of the funnel, not the end. The moment the product arrives at a customer's door is the highest-trust moment in the entire relationship. A good unboxing experience, a thoughtful follow-up message, and a simple referral ask at the right moment can triple the value of that customer.

The sustainable consumer goods category is at an inflection point in India. Awareness is rising faster than supply of trustworthy brands. The brands that build honest funnels — that educate first, sell second, and retain always — will own the next decade of this market.
